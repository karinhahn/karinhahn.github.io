My dream was not what I thought it was

For years, I had dreamt of one thing only. A dog. I was convinced that this was what was missing from life, I was so wrong. Here are the mistakes I made.

It started simply enough—browsing adoption sites at 2 AM, following every golden retriever account on Instagram, telling strangers at the park that I was "just visiting." I had the name picked out, the bed bought, the vet researched. I was ready.

What I wasn't ready for was the realization that the dream of a dog was really a dream of something else entirely. It was a dream of routine, of morning walks that forced me outside before the world got too loud, of unconditional company on the couch at night. The dog was never really about the dog.

What I actually needed

It took getting the dog to understand this. Don't get me wrong—I love him fiercely, this ridiculous creature who steals socks and is afraid of the toaster. But the peace I thought he'd bring? That had to come from somewhere else.
