Coffee, Rain, and Half-Finished Books

My bookshelf is a graveyard of good intentions. Dog-eared pages mark the exact moment life got in the way—or perhaps the moment the book stopped getting in the way of life. Either way, there's something honest about an unfinished story.

I count seventeen bookmarks scattered across the shelf. Some are proper bookmarks—the leather one from that shop in Portland, the pressed flower from my grandmother's garden. Others are receipts, gum wrappers, a torn corner of newspaper. Each one a tiny time capsule of who I was when I stopped reading.

The rain helps. On days like today, when the sky is the color of an old photograph, I pull one off the shelf at random and try again. Sometimes the words feel different. Sometimes I'm different. Sometimes the coffee gets cold before I notice, and that's how I know it's a good one.
