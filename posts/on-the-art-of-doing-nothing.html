On the Art of Doing Nothing

There's a peculiar kind of guilt that accompanies stillness. We've been conditioned to believe that every moment must be productive, every hour accounted for. But what if the most radical act of our time is simply to pause?

I've been thinking about this a lot lately. The way we fill silence with noise, the way we scroll through our phones in waiting rooms, the way "busy" has become our default answer to "how are you?"

The greatest threat to our well-being is not that we do too little, but that we never stop long enough to ask why we do what we do.

Last weekend, I sat on my porch for two hours with nothing but a cup of tea. No book, no phone, no agenda. Just me, the tea, and the sound of birds I'd never noticed before. It felt transgressive. It felt necessary.

Maybe doing nothing is its own kind of something.
